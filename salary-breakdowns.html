Current Members

The base salary for an Australian politician was increased to $203,030 per annum on 1 July 2017.

Politicians can earn additional income for taking on extra responsibilities, such as acting as a whip or chairing a committee.

An electorate allowance is also provided to each politician of at least $32,000 to cover costs incurred while performing their duties, with the unspent amount treated as taxable income.

A politician may also choose to take an extra $19,500 electorate allowance in lieu of a private-plated car.
